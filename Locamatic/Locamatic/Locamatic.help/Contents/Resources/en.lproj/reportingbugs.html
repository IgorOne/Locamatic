Reporting Bugs

So you found a bug? Excellent work! I'm delighted. Of course, now you have to get your specimen into the lab so that it can be classified and examined thoroughly.

The first thing to do is to fire up your email program and write an e-mail that accurately describes the nature of your bug:

  • Did the program crash?
  • Did the icon disappear from the menubar (assuming that you didn't explicitly hide it, of course)?
  • Did the icon remain in the menubar - but unresponsive to your clicks?

If you're a geek (or if you're feeling brave), you could fire up Console (it's an app in the Utilities folder of your Mac) and send me the error logs relating to Locamatic.

Next, describe the circumstances of the crash.

  • Did the crash happen immediately?
  • Did it happen when another program was loaded?
  • Does it happen when you connect to a particular network, or select a particular printer or homepage?
  • How long had Locamatic been running when the crash occurred?
  • How long had your Mac been running when the crash occurred?

Remember, this isn't an exhaustive list of the information that you need include in your crash report. This is just a list of a few things to consider when filing your report.

Finally, include details of your computer:

  • The model of your Mac.
  • The amount of memory that your Mac has.
  • The size of your hard drive, and the available free space.
  • The version of Mac OS that you're running - and please be as precise as you can.

You can find this information in the Apple Menu, About this Mac.

Send your completed bug report to support@45rpmsoftware.com.

In return, I will carefully examine your bug report, get back to you for more information if I require it, and fix the problem. I promise that I won't drop your email on the floor and ignore it.
